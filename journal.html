Journal Philosophy

The purpose of an investment journal is not simply to record events. It is to improve decision quality over time.

Writing creates clarity. By documenting investment ideas, research conclusions, portfolio decisions and lessons learned, it becomes easier to identify strengths, weaknesses and recurring patterns.

The journal serves as a long-term archive of intellectual development, helping transform experience into practical investing knowledge.

Why Keep An Investment Journal

Most investment mistakes are forgotten over time. Recording decisions creates accountability and allows future review of both successes and failures.

A written record helps separate process quality from investment outcomes. Good decisions can occasionally lead to poor results, while poor decisions can sometimes appear successful in the short term.

The objective is to evaluate thinking rather than simply evaluate outcomes.

Continuous Learning

Successful investing requires continuous adaptation. Markets evolve, industries change and new information constantly becomes available.

The objective of this journal is to remain intellectually curious, challenge assumptions and refine investment frameworks through ongoing study.

Learning is viewed not as a separate activity but as an essential component of long-term capital allocation.

Long-Term Objective

The long-term objective of this journal is to create a transparent record of intellectual development and investment decision-making.
